about.md

Engineer at the boundary of code and security.

A short story of who I am, how I work, and what kinds of problems get me out of bed.

I'm Rustem Nuryev — a backend & infrastructure engineer from Ashgabat, Turkmenistan. For the last four years I've been building APIs, wiring up networks, automating deployments, and occasionally taking a flashlight to systems to find what shouldn't be there.

What I love about IT is the full stack of reality behind every feature: the kernel, the packets on the wire, the firewall rules, the database query plan, the CI pipeline, and the CVE that ruined someone's weekend. I like understanding all of it, not just the framework on top.

My approach is simple: security-first, automate everything, write code you can read at 3 AM. I prefer boring, observable systems over clever ones. I write a lot of Bash, sometimes more than I should. I read RFCs for fun and audit my own code like a stranger wrote it.

Right now I'm open to interesting work — backend, network engineering, DevOps, or offensive/defensive security roles. If you have a system that needs to be built — or one you're worried about — let's talk.
